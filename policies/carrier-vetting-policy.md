## 1. Purpose

{{COMPANY_NAME}} adopts this policy to make carrier selection decisions that are consistent,
risk-based, documented, and explainable **based on the information available at the time**.
Motor carriers remain responsible for driver qualification, supervision, vehicle maintenance,
hours of service, and safe operation — this policy governs *selection*, not carrier operations.

## 2. Scope

Baseline: interstate, long-haul, full truckload. **Enhanced-risk freight** (reefer, high-value,
hazmat, pharma, electronics, alcohol/tobacco, luxury/theft-sensitive, expedited, team,
oversized) gets heightened review. Other modes (LTL, drayage, hotshot, final-mile) follow the
same principles with tailored rules.

## 3. Hard stops — never eligible for a load-by-load exception

No employee may proceed past a hard stop because of customer pressure, a tight market, a
cheap carrier, or a hard-to-cover load.

1. No active FMCSA operating authority for the transportation arranged
2. Carrier under an **out-of-service order**
3. **Conditional or Unsatisfactory** safety rating
4. Required auto/cargo insurance can't be verified, is inactive, or doesn't apply to the
   equipment, commodity, or lane
5. Carrier **identity cannot be reasonably verified**
6. Known or unresolved impersonation, fraud, cargo theft, unauthorized re-brokering,
   **double brokering** (a "sister company" hauling the load IS double brokering), or
   identity compromise
7. Carrier internally marked **Declined / Suspended / Do Not Use**
8. Unresolvable mismatch at pickup/paperwork/payment (carrier name, DOT/MC, truck, driver,
   BOL, POD, lumper receipt, invoice, factoring entity)
9. Cannot reasonably confirm the carrier accepting the load is the carrier physically hauling it
10. Carrier fails the adopted safety-data rule (§4)

## 4. Safety data rule (adopted standard — set your numbers, then never bend them per-load)

- **Safety rating:** Satisfactory or Unrated = eligible with full review. Conditional /
  Unsatisfactory = hard stop. (Unrated is normal — keep analyzing inspections/BASICs.)
- **BASIC thresholds** (common industry reference points — set yours, stricter is defensible):

  | BASIC category | Threshold ({{ADJUST}}) |
  |---|---|
  | Unsafe Driving | 65+ |
  | Controlled Substances / Alcohol | 65+ |
  | Hours of Service | 65+ |
  | Vehicle Maintenance | 80+ |
  | Driver Fitness | 80+ |

- **Disqualification rule:** a carrier over threshold in **{{2 or 3}}+ categories may not be
  used.** This is hard stop #10.
- **Crash data:** raw crash counts are not a stand-alone pass/fail — but material crash
  information that reaches you must be reviewed and documented.
- **OOS rates:** an OOS *order* is a hard stop; OOS *rates* are contextual indicators.
- Changing these numbers = a **documented, prospective policy change** (leadership review,
  written rationale, effective date, training) — never a one-load waiver.

## 5. Baseline requirements for ordinary approval

| Requirement | Rule | On a miss |
|---|---|---|
| Active authority + no OOS order | verified before tender | Hard stop |
| Insurance | ≥ ${{1,000,000}} auto liability / ${{100,000}} cargo, active and applicable; **scheduled-auto/driver policies → confirm the assigned truck/VIN/driver is on the policy** | Hard stop if unverifiable |
| Signed broker-carrier agreement + W-9 | on file | Obtain first |
| **Authority age** | ≥ **90 days continuous** (under 30 days = senior approval only, one-load limit, enhanced verification; 30–89 days = documented review incl. talking to the owner about prior operating experience) | Exception review |
| **Roadside inspections** | ≥ **1 completed inspection in prior 24 months**; sanity-check inspection count vs fleet size and volume | Escalate — never "ordinary capacity" |
| **New-carrier volume** | one **trial load** until clean pickup/delivery/POD/tracking/payment review | — |
| **Identity** | verify via FMCSA-reported contact info; never tender off an unverified dispatcher, email, or text | Hard stop if unresolved |
| **Chameleon screen** | shared principals/addresses/phones/emails/factoring with fraud, theft, DNU, or dead-authority entities | Escalate |
| **Shipment suitability** | carrier fits THIS commodity, value, lane, equipment, customer requirement | Enhanced review |

## 6. Double-brokering & pickup controls (where practical)

Hold the pickup number until the carrier/driver confirms shipment details · confirm driver
name/phone and the DOT/MC on the truck door · ask the shipper to verify carrier + DOT/MC at
pickup and **not to load on a mismatch** · require live ELD tracking when risk calls for it ·
watch volume-vs-fleet-size anomalies · resistance to tracking is a flag.

## 7. Actual knowledge — operational red flags (train every desk)

Escalate immediately: carrier/DOT/BOL/POD/payment-entity mismatch · driver doesn't know the
carrier or load · driver out of hours, refuses or disables tracking · shipper reports unsafe
equipment or driver confusion · **payment/factoring/remittance change request during or after
the shipment** (pause payment + investigate) · anyone mentions a "sister company" · notes or
calls indicating fraud, theft, or double brokering.

Response: **pause when practical → investigate → escalate → document → decide**
(proceed / restrict / suspend). Passing onboarding earlier is not a defense.

## 8. Exceptions — the 4-question note

Exceptions exist for **non-hard-stop baseline misses only** (authority age, inspection
history, volume limits). Every exception is recorded (`templates/exception-note.md`) and
answers: **(1)** what was the non-disqualifying risk · **(2)** what did we review · **(3)** what
facts mitigated it · **(4)** why was use reasonable + controls imposed + approver name/role/scope.

Never acceptable: "needed capacity" · "customer pressure" · "rep knows the carrier" ·
"had authority and insurance" (when other material risk existed). Exceptions may be approved
only by {{EXCEPTION_APPROVERS}} — never by anyone with a financial incentive on the load.

## 9. Monitoring & re-vet

Monitor active carriers on a defined cadence ({{e.g. new/occasional carriers = every load;
regulars = monthly}}). **Re-vet before use** after: 120 days of inactivity · insurance lapse ·
authority change · safety deterioration · fraud alert · ownership/contact/factoring change ·
payment anomaly · volume inconsistent with fleet size. Timestamp every check; append, never
overwrite.

## 10. What this policy does NOT require

You do not audit driver qualification files, run driver background checks, mechanically
inspect vehicles, dictate routes, or supervise the manner and means of transportation —
doing so can create its own liability. But an obvious problem actually seen (fake documents,
mismatched equipment, a driver who can't identify the load) must be escalated and documented.

## 11. Records & training

Retain vetting records, COIs, agreements, exception notes, safety snapshots, and payment
investigation notes **at least 4 years**. Train everyone who touches a load — sales, ops,
tracking, AP — on this policy; any of them may see the red flag first.
